God Defines Goodness

Kind of a continuation of [[good-news|my previous post]] where I stated that it would be "more good" for God to not exist, than for the majority of humans to suffer eternal conscious torment. A common argument against that idea would be that God, being the creator and sustainer of all things, has the authority to define "goodness" however God chooses.

The claim being, since God created reality, whatever God deems must be inherently good. That we, as sinners, all deserve the eternal punishment, and that God choosing to save even just a few of us is much more than we deserve. If we as humans disagree, that is a failing of our own limited perspective.

If we assume that God did indeed create all things, and that whatever God chooses is what is good, then one of the remaining variables we can play with is the number of people God chooses to save.1

I know of no traditions that suggest God will save no one. This makes sense for a variety of reasons. Such as, why bother spending time thinking about or worshiping a being who has no plans for you (or anyone for that matter) other than pain? And also, it just doesn't seem right, right? Can we really lump everyone together as equally bad or unredeemable? No, that can't be it, God must save at least some of us. And I don't think that is a heretical claim, as every group thinks they are the ones who are in the clear.

So we have established a baseline of goodness that God must adhere to, otherwise how can God really be good at all? It is clearly more "good" to save some rather than none.

And yet... why stop at just some? Why not most? Would that not be even more good? And it is completely within God's power to do so. It's not like God redeeming us takes effort or anything, seeing as God is all powerful and wholly in control of reality at its very core.

And yet... why stop at most? You see what I'm getting at?

It could be argued that salvation for all would go against God's desire for justice -- that God isn't only wholly loving, but also wholly just. But, if you think about it, "justice" is just other term that God can freely define at will. And so what is to stop God from saving everyone and saying that is what is good and just? The correct answer is nothing, as, according to orthodoxy, there is no power above God.

  1. Some might take umbrage with my phrasing here -- that God might not necessarily "choose" who to save, but instead we are all provided with adequate resources to make the choice for ourselves. This is a fair point, and something I will most likely address in a future post.↩︎
